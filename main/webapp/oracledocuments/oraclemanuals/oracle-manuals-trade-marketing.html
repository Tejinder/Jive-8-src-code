Illicit Trade Monitoring

Not Mandatory, but if method is to be conducted, it should be applied as described in this database.

Overview

Illicit trading in cigarette products represents a significant and growing threat to the Tobacco Industry. Markets need to be able to estimate the size of the illicit segment in order to determine the financial impact it has on the company, the industry, the trade and on government revenue. BAT has recently updated the Illicit Trade Tracking toolkit to offer a range of methods suited for this purpose depending on the market conditions.
Typically the frequency for GCS varies between quarterly and twice per year. GCS is a mandatory Common Platform survey for all T40 markets and is additionally governed by the Global Questionnaire and guidelines set by Global GCS Team each year.

Objective

The objectives behind the monitoring of illicit trade in cigarette products are:
  • To measure the size and nature of the trade in terms of the incidence and relative share of illicit cigarette products/brands.
  • To provide accurate annual estimation
  • To capture trendPurchasing Behaviour
  • To evaluate effectiveness of anti-illicit trade action
  • To derive financial impact of illicit trade on legitimate business. This requires brand level data.
